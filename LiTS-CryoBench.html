LiTS-CryoBench

LiTS-CryoBench is a benchmark dataset for personalized liver tumor cryoablation planning, constructed from a curated subset of the public LiTS dataset.

Liver Tumor Cryoablation Surgical Planning Benchmark Dataset

Overview

LiTS-CryoBench is designed to support research on image-guided liver tumor cryoablation planning, shifting the focus from pure segmentation tasks to clinically relevant planning objectives. The dataset includes contrast-enhanced abdominal CT scans with corresponding liver and tumor annotations inherited from LiTS, and is further curated to ensure suitability for planning-oriented studies.

All cases have been anonymized and formatted to facilitate reproducible research. The benchmark enables systematic comparison of planning methods under realistic anatomical constraints.

Data Content

  • Contrast-enhanced abdominal CT scans
  • Liver segmentation masks
  • Liver tumor segmentation masks
  • Preprocessed data suitable for cryoablation planning

Modality: CT
File format: NIfTI (.nii / .nii.gz)
Anatomical region: Liver

Intended Use

LiTS-CryoBench is intended for research on:

  • Cryoablation probe placement and optimization
  • Ablation zone modeling and simulation
  • Safety margin assessment
  • Planning evaluation and benchmarking

The dataset is not intended for clinical diagnosis or treatment.

Benchmark Purpose

Unlike existing liver tumor datasets that primarily target detection or segmentation, LiTS-CryoBench provides a standardized evaluation platform for cryoablation planning algorithms. It aims to bridge the gap between medical image analysis and interventional planning research.
